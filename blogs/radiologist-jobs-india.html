1. Scope of Radiologist Jobs in India

Radiologist jobs in India are in high demand due to several factors:

  • Increasing Diagnostic Imaging: CT scans, MRIs, and ultrasounds are becoming routine diagnostic tools.
  • Hospital Expansion: Corporate hospital chains are opening new facilities across Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities.
  • Tele-radiology Growth: Remote reporting creates new opportunities for radiologists to work from anywhere.
  • Aging Population: India's elderly population requires more diagnostic imaging for age-related diseases.

For context on other medical specializations, check out our Medical Specialization Guide 2026.

2. Qualifications & Training Path for Radiologists

To pursue radiologist jobs in India, you need:

  • MBBS (5.5 years): Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery from a recognized medical college.
  • MD Radiology (3 years): Doctor of Medicine in Radiodiagnosis or DNB Radiology.
  • State Medical Registration: Valid registration with State Medical Council or NMC.
  • Super-specialization (optional): DM Neuro Radiology or Fellowship in Interventional Radiology.

NEET PG Required: Admission to MD Radiology is through NEET PG. Top ranks are required for premier institutes like AIIMS, PGI Chandigarh, and JIPMER.

7. Career Growth & Super-Specialization

Radiologist jobs offer excellent career progression:

  • Junior Radiologist → Consultant → Senior Consultant → HOD → Director of Radiology
  • Super-specializations: Neuro Radiology, Interventional Radiology, Musculoskeletal Radiology, Pediatric Radiology
  • Academic Careers: Teaching positions in medical colleges as Assistant Professor → Professor
  • Private Practice: Own diagnostic center or partnership with existing centers

Emerging Opportunity: Tele-radiology is growing rapidly. Radiologists can work from home reporting scans for hospitals in remote locations or even international clients.

AI in Radiology: Threat or Opportunity?

Artificial intelligence is transforming radiology. AI algorithms can detect abnormalities in X-rays and CT scans with high accuracy. However, radiologist jobs are not disappearing — they're evolving. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the salary of a radiologist in India?

Radiologist salary in India ranges from ₹12L to ₹40L per annum. Freshers earn ₹12L-18L, experienced radiologists earn ₹20L-30L, and senior consultants earn ₹30L-50L+ annually.

Which hospitals hire radiologists in India?

Top hospitals hiring radiologists include Apollo Hospitals, Fortis Healthcare, Max Healthcare, Manipal Hospitals, Narayana Health, AIIMS, and Tata Memorial Hospital. Diagnostic chains also hire radiologists.

What qualifications are required for radiologist jobs?

Complete MBBS (5.5 years) followed by MD Radiology (3 years) or DNB Radiology. Super-specialization like DM Neuro Radiology is optional but adds value.

Is MD Radiology a good career choice?

Yes. MD Radiology offers excellent salary, work-life balance (minimal emergencies), and diverse practice settings — from hospitals to diagnostic centers to tele-radiology.
